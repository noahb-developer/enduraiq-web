Help & Getting Started

Everything you need to get the most out of EnduraIQ.

📥 Exporting from Garmin Connect
1
Go to connect.garmin.com on your computer (browser, not the app).
2
Click Activities in the left menu, then click any workout you want to analyze.
3
Click the ⚙️ gear icon in the top right of the activity page.
4
Click Export to CSV. A file downloads to your computer.
5
Go to Analyze and upload the CSV. Done.
🎯 Finding your LTHR

LTHR (Lactate Threshold Heart Rate) is the single most important number for accurate zones. Here's the standard 30-minute field test:

1
Warm up thoroughly for 15 minutes.
2
Do a 30-minute solo time trial at the hardest pace you can sustain (run or bike, separately).
3
Your average HR for the last 20 minutes is your LTHR.

Don't want to test? Use defaults in Settings. You can update anytime.

Frequently asked questions
What sports does EnduraIQ support?
Currently: running, cycling, and pool swimming. More sports (rowing, cross-country skiing) coming later.
Do I need a Garmin watch?
Any GPS watch that exports CSV works (Garmin, COROS, Polar, Suunto). Garmin is most common and best supported.
Is my data private?
Yes. Your data is stored in your browser and on your account only. We never share or sell training data. Ever.
How accurate are the analyses?
The analysis uses published sports science: Friel LTHR zones, cardiac drift research, and acute:chronic workload ratio models. Accuracy depends on the accuracy of YOUR inputs — especially your LTHR. Test it properly.
What's the difference between EnduraIQ and Strava?
Strava is a social network for activities. EnduraIQ is a coaching analyzer. We do one thing — turn your data into coaching decisions. They do many things, but coaching isn't one of them.
Can I connect Garmin automatically?
Currently no — you upload CSV files manually. Automatic Garmin sync is coming in v2 (pending Garmin developer approval).
What does it cost?
Free tier: unlimited analyses, 7 days of history. Pro ($15/mo, launching later): unlimited history, trend analysis, race planning, injury risk scoring, automatic Garmin sync.
